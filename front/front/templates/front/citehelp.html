Citing Densho Articles

American Psychological Association (APA)

[Author last name], [Author first (and middle if available) initial]. ([Date published]). [Article name]. Densho Encyclopedia. Retrieved on [Date retrieved] from [URL].

Example:
Odo, F. (2012). Varsity Victory Volunteers. Densho Encyclopedia. Retrieved on July 31, 2015 from https://encyclopedia.densho.org/Varsity%20Victory%20Volunteers/.

If no listed author:
Varsity Victory Volunteers. (2012). Densho Encyclopedia. Retrieved on July 31, 2015 from https://encyclopedia.densho.org/Varsity%20Victory%20Volunteers/.

For further information:
http://www.apastyle.org/learn/quick-guide-on-references.aspx#Websites

Modern Language Association (MLA) Style

[Author last name], [Author first name]. "[Article name]." Densho Encyclopedia. Densho, [Date published]. Web. [Date retrieved].

Example:
Odo, Franklin. "Varsity Victory Volunteers." Densho Encyclopedia. Densho, 2012. Web. 31 July 2015.

If no listed author:
"Varsity Victory Volunteers." Densho Encyclopedia. Densho, 2012. Web. 31 July 2015.

For further information:
http://owl.english.purdue.edu/owl/resource/747/08/

Chicago Manual of Style

[Author last name], [Author first name]. "[Article name]." Densho Encyclopedia ([Date of publication]) Accessed [Date retrieved]. [URL].

Example:
Odo, Franklin. "Varsity Victory Volunteers." Densho Encyclopedia (2012). Accessed July 31, 2015. https://encyclopedia.densho.org/Varsity%20Victory%20Volunteers/.

If no listed author:
"Varsity Victory Volunteers." Densho Encyclopedia (2012). Accessed July 31, 2015. https://encyclopedia.densho.org/Varsity%20Victory%20Volunteers/.

For further information:
http://www.chicagomanualofstyle.org/tools_citationguide.html

Modern Humanities Research Association (MHRA) Style

[Author last name], [Author first name], '[Article name]', Densho Encyclopedia ([Date of publication]) <[URL]> [accessed [Date retrieved]].

Example:
Odo, Franklin, 'Varsity Victory Volunteers', Densho Encyclopedia (2012) <https://encyclopedia.densho.org/Varsity%20Victory%20Volunteers/> [accessed 31 July 2015].

If no listed author:
'Varsity Victory Volunteers', Densho Encyclopedia (2012) <https://encyclopedia.densho.org/Varsity%20Victory%20Volunteers/> [accessed 31 July 2015].

For further information:
http://www.mhra.org.uk/Publications/Books/StyleGuide/download.shtml http://www.cardiff.ac.uk/insrv/resources/guides/but028.pdf

Council of Science Editors (CSE) Style [Formerly Council of Biology Editors (CBE)]

[Author last name] [Author first (and middle if available) initial]. [Article name] Densho Encyclopedia [Internet]. [Date published]. [cited [Date retrieved]]. Available from [URL].

Example:
Odo F. Varsity Victory Volunteers. Densho Encyclopedia [Internet]. 2012 [cited 2015 July 31]. Available from: https://encyclopedia.densho.org/Varsity%20Victory%20Volunteers/.

If no listed author:
Varsity Victory Volunteers. Densho Encyclopedia [Internet]. 2012 [cited 2015 July 31]. Available from: https://encyclopedia.densho.org/Varsity%20Victory%20Volunteers/.

For further information:
http://www.libraries.psu.edu/psul/lls/students/cse_citation.html

Bluebook style

[Author first name] [Author last name], [Article name], DENSHO ENCYCLOPEDIA, ([Date published]), [URL].

Example:
Franklin Odo, Varsity Victory Volunteers, DENSHO ENCYCLOPEDIA, (2012), https://encyclopedia.densho.org/Varsity%20Victory%20Volunteers/.

If no listed author:
Varsity Victory Volunteers, DENSHO ENCYCLOPEDIA, (2012), https://encyclopedia.densho.org/Varsity%20Victory%20Volunteers/.

For further information:
http://libraryguides.nesl.edu/content.php?pid=358326&sid=2934955#inet
